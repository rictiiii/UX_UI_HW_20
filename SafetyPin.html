Safety Pin

JULY, 2020 | UI&UX DESIGN | PERSONAL PROJECT

What

Safety Pin is an app that will provide emergency contact information to travelers and local reviews/tips for safety precautions in areas people are traveling to.

Why

People are in need of a resource that can provide safety precautions and tips in local area they are traveling in.

User Research

Beginning with conducting 5 interviews in order to discover what safety concerns our users had and resources they would like to have regarding their safety issues. We discovered that there was a high priority on a resource that can provide in depth details of safe places to explore, local laws, and emergency contact information.

I surveyed 24 participants, ages 21-45, who on average stated that safety is a high priority while they travel and they would like to know of potential dangers when traveling to their specific destination. Some of my interviewees expressed safety tips they would like to receive and current travel concerns they may have while traveling.

Definition

A user persona was created — Priscilla Perez, who is an adventurous traveler. She needs an online platform that prevents unsafe situations when she travels, because safety is very important to her travel experience, she wants to get notifications and tips in order to avoid unsafe areas and activities.

Safety Pin should be designed to prevent travelers from encountering unsafe areas and activities, I have observed that travelers don’t usually have enough knowledge about the place they visit, and often walk in unsafe areas without knowing. How might we design Safety Pin so that when people travel in an unfamiliar city, they can gather safety tips and get alerts while they’re wandering around?

Ideation

After understanding the user insight, I started brainstorming, and generated quick ideas that could help the users with their pain points. I prioritized the ideas using a 2x2 matrix. From the matrix, I identified a few ideas my app should focus on:

1. Safety Pin should provide a platform for people to share their travel experience

2. Notify users when they’re entering unsafe areas

3. Allow local residents to provide tips for travelers

4. Gather safety tips and present to users

Prototyping

I made competitor analysis on 4 apps: Smart Traveler, TripLingo, Lyft, and bSafe. From which I found inspirations, as well as problems I should avoid. I satarted drawing sketches, and then creating digital wireframs based on the drafts.

User Testing + Iteration

In this session, I created Guerrilla Testing plan, in order to identify pain points in the process of gathering safety tips on Safety Pin. Based on the testing feedback, I added Setting and Profile pages, more coaching screens about different functions, avoided re-login after registration and resetting password, as well as improving other lower prioritised functions.

Finish Product

In this project, I got to explore the pain points travelers generally have, and through my empathy towards them, I designed Safety Pin. This app helps them navigate through unsafe areas and provides emergency solutions.

During the process, I learned that researching and creating surveys for the target customers is important, as I found new/surprising ideas about what specific problems I could focus on. As a UX designer, it is crucial to have empathy for the customer’s pain points, and to create solutions from innovative, unique perspectives.
