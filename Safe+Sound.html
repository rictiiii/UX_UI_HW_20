Safe + Sound

JANUARY, 2021 | UI&UX DESIGN | TEAM PROJECT

What

A drive-in music festival experience that provides designated entry time, socially distanced area to enjoy the show, food and drinks delivered with drones, and a new way to connect with people.

Why

During the COVID-19 pandemic, people are bored with staying home all the time. There are few options to have fun and socialize safely.

User Research

We started our research with our proto persona: Zane Hassel, a 25 year old party animal whose tired of restrictions and needs to find a way to let loose and rock out. From there, we created and sent out a survey and discovered the majority of people missed having fun/ social activities (As we suspected) and that the top no-contact methods for concerts people prefered are staying in vehicles and fenced- areas for social distancing.

Our team belived creating a drive-in festival could be the solution to these frustrations. We researched potential competitors, and they gave us inspiration as to what to include in our own designs: A desktop design to order tickets and get important faqs and directions and An app to use during the festival to order food and socialize.

We compiled our research and created the user persona: Zoey Roberts, a 29 yr old influencer whose passionate about festivals and frustrated with the current no-contact music event options. She’s on the search for an event thats fun and safe and lets her hear that bass drop.

Definition

Zoey is searching for a safe and exciting no-contact music festival that is just as engaging as pre-pandemic events where she can have fun and socialize safely. One where COVID safety measures are implemented in a way that will keep her safe but won't prevent her from having a good time.

People like Zoey are generally tired of COVID restrictions and have exhausted all of their at-home entertainment options. While they are still very concerned about their health and safety during the pandemic, they are searching for a safe, engaging, smoothly run event where COVID safety measures are implemented in a way that will keep them safe but won't prevent them from having a good time.

How might we create a website/app that provides information about a no-contact music festival while reassuring attendees that the event will be safe, efficient and entertaining so that those seeking excitement during the pandemic will be motivated enough to attend a no-contact festival?

Ideation

We brainstormed and decided on 3 key features to include in our design:

• Ordering food from our app and have it delivered by drone

• 360 degree stage

• Designating numbers for each parking area so attendees can communicate with each other and send food/drinks

2 user flows for the app:

Drone Food Delivery -— We believe using drones to deliver food and drinks can avoid contacts and large gatherings, therefore ensure the users’ safety

Concert Chat -— Even though users are required to stay in their designated area, they can still use this function to socialize with new people in the festival! They can simply find the person they’re interested in through the map, and decide to send them a message or a drink

Prototyping

Here’s the lo-fi version of the concert chat user flow. Zoey sees a boy she would like to talk to. She clicks on his parking space number, then clicks on his profile. She initiates a chat and sends him a drink

Later, Zoey decides to order food for herself. She clicks on the food icon in the bottom bar, browses the food menu, makes her selection and pays with her saved credit card that she setup when buying tickets. Her food is then delivered to her parking space via drone

User Testing + Iteration

Some iterations we made based on feedback from user testing:

• We re-designed the ordering process in the food flow to have the user select the food items first, then confirm item quantity on a pushup screen

• We also added the recipient’s UserName to the bottom of the Order Received message to make it clear who the order was being sent to

• We added status icons to profiles so users could indicate if they were available to chat with other attendees and went through a few versions of the user profile screen - ending up with the version on the bottom left with the “Send Food/Drink” button option
