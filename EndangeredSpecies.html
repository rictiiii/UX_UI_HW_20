Endangered Species

SEPTEMBER, 2020 | UI&UX DESIGN | PERSONAL PROJECT

What

Redesign UX & UI or Endangered Spicies goverment website, in order for users to find information they need efficiantly and enjoyble.

Why

The website contains confusing information architecture and unappealing aesthetic. Difficult to navigate through the heavy amount of content and find needed information.

User Research

During the research process, I developed a proto persona-Michael Mckinley, a 38 year old guy who likes to hunt. He wants to find out what potentially threatened species live in his area, so he can hunt responsibly.

Michael's user path on the current Endangered Species website contains 4 pages, after having heuristic evaluations and usability tests, I concluded a few pain points that would be the focus of my redesign:

• Hierachy - difficult to navigate through the heavy and poorly organized content to find useful information

• Visual Presentation - the current design and layout isn't applealing, and it doesn't speak anything about the endangered species

• Search Bars - many testers complained that the search bars have miss functions and placements

• Pictures - there're different pages missing pictures or using unhelpful pictures

Navigation Prototype

I analyzed the current navigation system, made card sorting, and recreated the sitemap. Based on the new information architecture, I moved onto the prototype for the navigation. The redesigned navigation was built based on “hierarchy” in LATCH principle, I arranged the categories from what’s more important for the users to what’s less important.

I believe with simplified visual language, users can navigate through a clear structure among this information heavy website.

Desktop Prototype

Based off of the user path, I honed in on 4 pages: home, search result, species detail, and zoomed in picture. I wanted to convey a combination of mystery and seriousness throughout my style guide and apply to this redesign.

After having some testing on the Lo-Fi prototype, I realized the hierachy on some pages could be improved. Based on the feedback I received, I created the Hi-Fi version for the desktop redesign.

Mobile + Testing

With the desktop version completed, the mobile version came along easily. During the tests, a few of my users pointed out they thought the navigation in the species page looked like a footer. I then changed it to a button so it doesn't create confusions.
